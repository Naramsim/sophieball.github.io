Call for Proposals: Can You Be More Creative Than ChatGPT?

Workshop on Large Language Models (LLMs) in Social Sciences and Humanities Research

Are you ready to demonstrate the unique creativity of human researchers? Join us for an online workshop on [insert date], where we invite innovative research proposals that leverage Large Language Models (LLMs) to push the boundaries of social sciences and humanities research. Alongside your submissions, we’ll also review GPT-generated proposals. Can your ideas outshine AI’s outputs?

The Challenge

We’re seeking proposals that creatively integrate LLMs, such as ChatGPT, BERT, or GPT-4, into research projects across diverse fields, including but not limited to:

  • Quantitative Studies: Automated content analysis, large-scale corpus studies.
  • Qualitative Research: Discourse analysis, thematic coding, interview synthesis.
  • Mixed-Methods Approaches: Combining computational and interpretive techniques.
  • Innovative Applications: Historical and cultural studies, policymaking, and survey analysis.

This workshop emphasizes conceptual novelty—no results are required. We encourage submissions that propose original frameworks, explore theoretical applications, or identify challenges in using LLMs.

Submission Guidelines

Please submit a 1-page proposal in PDF format, adhering to the following:

  • Length: Maximum of 1 page, single-spaced.
  • Font: 10-point size.
  • Figures/Tables: Include only one figure OR one table.
  • References: Follow IEEE citation style.
  • Content:
    • Title: Clear and concise.
    • Concept: Briefly describe the research question or goal.
    • Methodology: Specify the role of LLMs (quantitative, qualitative, or mixed-methods).
    • Potential Impact: Highlight the significance of the proposed idea.
    • Challenges and Ethics: Discuss potential barriers and ethical considerations.
